You have: Chronic Cholestasis

Description:

Chronic cholestasis is a long-term condition where the flow of bile from the liver is reduced or blocked. Bile is essential for digestion and absorption of fats and fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K). When bile builds up, it can damage liver cells and lead to scarring or cirrhosis.

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice)
  • Itchy skin
  • Fatigue
  • Pain in the upper right side of the abdomen
  • Dark urine
  • Pale stools

Causes:

  • Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC) – autoimmune destruction of bile ducts
  • Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) – inflammation and scarring of bile ducts
  • Bile duct obstruction – from gallstones, tumors, or strictures
  • Certain medications – like anabolic steroids or antibiotics
  • Genetic/metabolic conditions – e.g., Alagille syndrome, cystic fibrosis

Treatment:

Ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA) – helps bile flow better and slows liver damage Obeticholic acid – for patients who don’t respond to UDCA (especially in PBC) Cholestyramine – relieves itching by binding bile acids in the gut Rifampin – antibiotic with antipruritic effects Antihistamines – sometimes used to ease itching Fat-soluble vitamin supplements (A, D, E, K) – to correct deficiencies Corticosteroids or immunosuppressants – used in certain autoimmune forms

Milk Thistle (Silymarin) – antioxidant properties may help protect liver cells Dandelion Root – traditionally used to support bile flow Artichoke Leaf Extract – may support liver and bile production Omega-3 fatty acids – reduce liver inflammation (from flaxseed or fish oil) Turmeric (Curcumin) – anti-inflammatory, supports liver function Probiotics – help improve gut-liver interaction and reduce toxin buildup

For more detailed information, consult your healthcare provider.
